The Bitcoin Trade Volume vs Transaction Volume Ratio and Bitcoin Market Price

The Bitcoin Trade Volume vs Transaction Volume Ratio and Bitcoin Market Price is an important metric to understand the market dynamics.
Trade Volume vs Volume Ratio: This metric represents the average value of a Bitcoin transaction. It is calculated by dividing the total market price of Bitcoin (MKPRU) by the total transaction volume (TXVOL).
Trends: The chart indicates that the ratio has experienced fluctuations over time. There have been periods of high ratios, suggesting larger average transaction values, and periods of lower ratios, indicating smaller average transaction values.
Bitcoin Market Price: This metric represents the price of Bitcoin at a given time. It is expressed in units of the local currency (e.g., USD).
Trends: The chart shows that the Bitcoin market price has been highly volatile, with significant price swings both upwards and downwards. There have been periods of rapid price increases followed by sharp declines.

Observations:

Correlation: There appears to be a general correlation between the two metrics. When the Bitcoin Market Price increases, the Trade Volume vs Volume Ratio tends to also increase, suggesting that larger transactions are more common during periods of price appreciation.
Volatility: Both metrics exhibit significant volatility, especially the Bitcoin Market Price, which is known for its price fluctuations.
Trend: The overall trend of both metrics is upward, indicating a general increase in both the price of Bitcoin and the average transaction value over the period analyzed.
